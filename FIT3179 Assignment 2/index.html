Suicide Rate in Europe
2000 - 2016

Suicide is a death that is caused by a person injuring and hurting themselves. Reasons could range from depression, drug overdose to physical abuse. Bonding and connecting with a person can help prevent and improve their mental health condition.

Suicide is heavily focused and emphasized on today's generation as the number of suicide rate is drastically increasing through out the years. The World Health Organization (WHO) study estimates every year, nearly 800,000 people dies from suicides.

Suicide Rate across Europe

This map of Europe shows us the each year's suicide rate which is suicide per 100k population some of the countries with a darker shade of red represent a high suicide rate in that country.

From the map we can see that the Eastern side of Europe has a higher suicide rate compared to the other regions in Europe.

(Note: Year 2016 data isn't completed. Data missing in some countries)

Correlation Between Suicide Rate and GDP per Capita

A bubble chart is used to determine if there is any correlation between suicide rate and GDP per Capita. As you can see from the chart on the left, there is a correlation as the higher the GDP per Capita, the suicide rate tend to be lower. Reasons could be people in those countries are more financially stable and don't have to stress about surviving day-to-day.

Countries from the Northern side of Europe tend to have lower average of suicide rate and higher average of GDP per Capita. Although countries from the North have a lower population count compared to the other regions. Lithuania in the year 2000 has the highest suicide rate with a total of 47.65.

(Note: There are many interactive features in the bubble chart. The legend of the regions are interactive as well!)

Difference between 2 Genders

Suicides are more common amongst men compare to women. From the normalized bar chart on the right we can see that the percentage of suicides in each year is dominated by men from the numbers. Each year the ratio between both genders and relatively the same.

From the area chart above, we can see that the trend of suicide rate throughout the years doesn't differ to much, but we do see that there is a slight decrease. The peak suicide rate is in the year 2000 with a value of 37.53. It is also the highest value for both men and female with 7.50 and 30.03 respectively.

Comparison between Generations

From the line chart we can see that the older generations has a higher suicide rate compared to the younger generations. Although we know that in this day of age the younger generation face more mental health issues.

We do have to note that the older generations such as Boomer, Silent and the G.I Generations have gone through world wars and that plays a big factor into numbers of death from suicides.

G.I Generations only shows up in the year 2000 as they have past the average life expectancy of 80 years old after the year 2000. Boomer shows the most suicide numbers as shown in the donut chart above.
